THE CRAZE FOR PLASTIC SURGERY IN THE US


Plastic surgery is a surgical procedure involving restoration, reconstruction, or alteration of the human body. It can be divided into two main categories: reconstructive surgery and cosmetic surgery.

The US has the highest percentage of plastic surgeries in the world, with nearly 5 million procedures done in 2020.


The map visualisation shows the Google Trends Relative Search Volumes for all searches for "plastic surgery" in each state in 2020.

The 2 cities with the least population: Vermont and Wyoming, have the most search for plastic surgery with around 10 out of 100,000 people looking up plastic surgeries in google search.

Big cities like California, Texas, and New York have the least search for plastic surgery with close to 0 people looking up plastic surgeries in google search.

However, keep in mind that searching for plastic surgery does not actually mean undergoing the procedure and high google search does not equal high demand.

Reconstructive Surgery

Reconstructive surgery is surgery performed to restore normal appearance and function to body parts malformed by a disease or medical condition.

The packed bubble shows the number of reconstructive procedures performed in 2020.

Tumor removal is the most popular procedure, at around 5.2 million procedures performed in 2020 alone.

The area chart shows the TOP 5 Reconstructive surgeries performed in the US in 2020.

There is an increasing trend in the number of Reconstructive surgeries performed in the US, peaking at 2020 with 6.88 million procedures performed despite the Covid-19 pandemic.

Cosmetic Surgery

Cosmetic surgery Cosmetic surgery is surgery performed to change the physical appearance for cosmetic rather than medical reasons.

Cosmetic surgery can be classified into 2 categories: surgical procedures and minimally invasive procedures. Minimally invasive procedures are smaller procedures that involve minimal incision compared to surgical procedures.

The area chart shows the total number of cosmetic surgeries performed in the US from 2010 to 2020.

There is an increasing trend in the total number of cosmetic surgeries in the US, peaking in 2019 with 18.4 million procedures before decreasing in 2020. Unlike reconstructive surgeries, cosmetic surgeries are not urgent and therefore, some offices closed down and hospitals halted cosmetic procedures in 2020, causing the decline.

Minimally invasive procedures are more popular than surgical procedures due to less scarring, less expensive, and most importantly less permanent impact on the body as some people are not ready to make a big permanent decision.

The tree map shows the total number of cosmetic surgery procedures done in the US in 2020, categorised into 3 body parts: head, torso, limbs.

Most people undergo cosmetic procedures on their head area due to society's emphasis on facial attractiveness more than body attractiveness.

Nose reshaping, also known as rhinoplasty, is the most popular cosmetic procedure with a whopping 353,000 procedures done in 2020 alone. The histogram shows the age distribution of plastic surgery patients in the US in 2020.

Most people undergo cosmetic surgery between the age of 40 - 54. People are turning to cosmetic surgeries as a way to look young, mostly minimally invasive procedures to reduce wrinkles and tightens the skin.

The pie chart shows female to male ratio of cosmetic surgery patients.

The cosmetic surgery industry is female dominated, with 92% of patients being female. Females tend to accept cosmetic surgery more than males due to the higher expectations regarding their physical appearance.

The most popular procedure among female is nose reshaping, with a whopping 287,000 procedures performed in 2020, followed closely by eyelid surgery with 279,000 procedures.

Like females, nose reshaping and eyelid surgery are the 2 most popular procedures among males with 65,100 and 45,800 procedures done in 2020 respectively.

Liposuction (the removal of fat under the skin) is also popular among both males and females due to the influence of social media on slim and fit bodies.
